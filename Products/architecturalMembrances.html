    Architectural Membrances

    • Architectural membranes are relatively new construction materials being used in India.
    • The applications of architectural membranes include construction of permanent and semi-permanent structures such as car park covers, cafes etc..
    • Architectural membranes are strong, energy efficient and aesthetically superior products that offer flexibility to designers and architects thus, allowing high levels of creativity.

    Advantages

    • Lightweight Construction:
    • Flexibility and Formability
    • Natural Light Transmission
    • Energy Efficiency
    • Acoustic Performance

    Specifications

    • Material Composition: Architectural membranes are typically made from PVC or PTFE-coated fabrics, chosen for their durability, weather resistance, and translucency
    • Tensile Strength: Membranes have high tensile strength to withstand tensioning forces and resist wind loads and other environmental stresses.
    • Fire Resistance: Membranes may be treated with fire-retardant additives to meet fire safety regulations and standards.
    • UV Stability: UV-resistant coatings or additives are often incorporated into architectural membranes to prevent degradation and ensure long-term performance in outdoor environments.
