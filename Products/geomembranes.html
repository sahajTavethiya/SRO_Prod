    Geomembrane

    • A geomembrane is very low permeability synthetic membrane liner or barrier used with any geotechnical engineering related material so as to control fluid (liquid or gas) migration in a human-made project, structure, or system.
    • Geomembranes are made from relatively thin continuous polymeric sheets, but they can also be made from the impregnation of geotextiles with asphalt, elastomer or polymer sprays, or as multilayered bitumen geocomposites. Continuous polymer sheet geomembranes are, by far, the most common.
    • The above represents approximately $1.8 billion in worldwide sales.
    • Projections for future geomembrane usage are strongly dependent on the application and geographical location.

    Advantages

    • Impermeability
    • Chemical Resistance
    • Durability
    • Flexibility
    • Environmental Protection

    Specifications

    • Material: Geomembranes are typically made from synthetic materials such as high-density polyethylene (HDPE),
    • Thickness: Geomembranes are available in a range of thicknesses, typically measured in millimeters or mils (1 mil = 0.0254 mm).
    • Tensile Strength: Tensile strength is the maximum load that a geomembrane can withstand without breaking when subjected to tension.
